Fundamentals of Surface Chemistry

Surface chemistry deals with the chemical reactions and phenomena that occur at the interfaces of materials. It plays a crucial role in catalysis, adsorption, and material science.

Adsorption

The process by which atoms, ions, or molecules from a gas, liquid, or dissolved solid adhere to a surface.

Surface Energy

Understanding the excess energy at the surface of a material compared to its bulk, influencing phenomena like wetting and adhesion.

Catalysis

The process of accelerating a chemical reaction through the use of a catalyst, often involving surface interactions.

Colloids and Interfaces

Study of colloidal systems and their interfaces, including stability, behavior, and applications in various fields.

Applications of Surface Chemistry

Surface chemistry has significant applications in fields like catalysis, pharmaceuticals, and environmental science.

Industrial Catalysts

Understanding the role of surface chemistry in the development and use of catalysts in industrial processes.

Drug Delivery Systems

Exploration of how surface chemistry influences drug release mechanisms and the design of nanocarriers.

Environmental Remediation

Application of surface chemistry in the removal of pollutants from the environment through adsorption techniques.

Coatings and Films

Study of surface treatments and coatings to enhance material properties such as corrosion resistance and wear protection.
