Newton's Laws of Motion

Newton's laws describe the relationship between the motion of an object and the forces acting on it. These laws form the foundation of classical mechanics.

First Law (Inertia)

An object will remain at rest or move in a straight line at constant speed unless acted upon by an external force.

Second Law (F=ma)

The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass.

Third Law (Action and Reaction)

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. Forces always act in pairs.

Friction and Its Effects

Friction is a force that opposes the relative motion between two surfaces in contact. It plays a significant role in controlling motion.
