Evolution

Explore the principles of evolution, natural selection, and the diversity of life essential for understanding biology and NEET preparation.

Understanding Evolution

Evolution is the process by which species change over time through variations, mutations, and natural selection. It explains the diversity of life on Earth and the relationships between different organisms.

Theory of Evolution

Overview of the historical context and foundational concepts of the theory of evolution, including key figures like Charles Darwin.

Natural Selection

Understanding the mechanism of natural selection and how it leads to adaptation and speciation.

Genetic Variation

Exploration of genetic variation within populations and its role in evolution through mutation and recombination.

Evidence of Evolution

Examination of various lines of evidence supporting evolution, including fossil records, comparative anatomy, and molecular biology.

Speciation and Extinction

Speciation refers to the process by which new species arise, while extinction is the end of an existing species. Both processes play significant roles in shaping biodiversity.

Mechanisms of Speciation

Understanding the processes that lead to the formation of new species, including allopatric and sympatric speciation.

Mass Extinctions

Overview of historical mass extinction events, their causes, and their impact on biodiversity and evolution.

Adaptive Radiation

Exploration of adaptive radiation as a process where organisms diversify rapidly into a variety of forms to adapt to different environments.

Human Evolution

Study of the evolutionary history of humans, including the emergence of Homo sapiens and our relationship to other primates.
