Types of Solutions

A solution is a homogeneous mixture composed of two or more substances. Understanding the types of solutions is crucial in chemistry.

Liquid Solutions

Mixtures where a solute is dissolved in a solvent, forming a clear liquid. Examples include saltwater and sugarwater.

Solid Solutions

Homogeneous mixtures where solid solutes are dispersed within solid solvents, such as alloys like bronze and brass.

Gas Solutions

Mixtures where gases mix uniformly, such as air, which is a solution of gases like nitrogen, oxygen, and carbon dioxide.

Aqueous Solutions

Solutions in which water is the solvent. Important in chemical reactions and biological processes.

Concentration of Solutions

Concentration refers to the amount of solute in a given volume of solvent or solution. Understanding concentration is vital for preparing solutions.

Molarity (M)

The number of moles of solute per liter of solution. It is a common unit of concentration used in chemistry.

Molality (m)

The number of moles of solute per kilogram of solvent. Useful for temperature-related properties.

Percentage Concentration

Expressing the concentration of a solution as a percentage of solute in a given volume or mass of solution.

Parts Per Million (PPM)

A way to express very dilute concentrations of substances, often used in environmental science.
