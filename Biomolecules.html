Biomolecules

Explore the chemistry of life by understanding the structure, function, and significance of biomolecules essential for NEET preparation.

Introduction to Biomolecules

Biomolecules are organic compounds that form the basis of living organisms. They play essential roles in structure, metabolism, and genetic information processing.

Carbohydrates

Study the structure and function of carbohydrates, which serve as primary sources of energy and structural components.

Proteins

Explore proteins, the workhorses of the cell, involved in catalysis, transport, signaling, and structural support.

Lipids

Understand the role of lipids as energy reserves, cell membrane components, and signaling molecules.

Nucleic Acids

Learn about DNA and RNA, the molecules responsible for genetic information storage and transmission.

Types and Functions of Biomolecules

Biomolecules vary in structure and function, contributing uniquely to life processes. Explore the diverse types and their essential roles.

Monosaccharides

Simple sugars like glucose and fructose, which serve as basic energy sources and building blocks.

Polysaccharides

Complex carbohydrates such as starch and cellulose, providing energy storage and structural integrity.

Enzymes

Proteins that act as biological catalysts, accelerating chemical reactions necessary for life processes.

Genetic Material

DNA and RNA, carriers of genetic information, responsible for inheritance and protein synthesis.
