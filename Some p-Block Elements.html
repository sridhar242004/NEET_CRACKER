Understanding p-Block Elements

The p-block of the periodic table includes groups 13 to 18, containing a variety of elements with diverse properties and applications.

Boron (B)

Boron is a metalloid with applications in glass and ceramics, and it is crucial in the production of borosilicate glass.

Carbon (C)

Carbon is essential for life, forming the basis of organic chemistry, and has applications in materials science.

Oxygen (O)

Oxygen is vital for respiration in living organisms and is involved in combustion processes.

Fluorine (F)

Fluorine is the most electronegative element, used in toothpaste and Teflon production.

Silicon (Si)

Silicon is a semiconductor used extensively in electronics and solar cells.

Nitrogen (N)

Nitrogen is essential for life, making up a large part of the Earth's atmosphere and used in fertilizers.
