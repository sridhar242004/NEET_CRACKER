Understanding Gases

Gases have unique properties that differentiate them from solids and liquids. They are compressible and expand to fill their container, with interactions between molecules being minimal.

Study of gas properties, including pressure, volume, temperature, and how they relate through gas laws.

Ideal Gas vs. Real Gas

Comparison of ideal gases with real gases, including deviations from ideal behavior at high pressures and low temperatures.

Applications of Gases

Exploration of the role of gases in various applications, such as in the atmosphere, industrial processes, and everyday life.

Understanding Liquids

Liquids possess distinct characteristics that set them apart from gases and solids. They have a definite volume but no definite shape, adapting to the shape of their container.

Properties of Liquids

Examination of properties like viscosity, surface tension, and cohesion in liquids.

Phase Changes

Understanding phase transitions, including evaporation, condensation, and freezing, and how they relate to temperature and pressure.

Liquid Behavior

Investigation of the behavior of liquids under different conditions, including hydrostatic pressure and buoyancy.

Applications of Liquids

Exploration of the importance of liquids in various fields, such as chemistry, biology, and engineering.
