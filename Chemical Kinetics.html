Introduction to Chemical Kinetics

Chemical kinetics is the study of reaction rates and the steps through which reactions occur. This field helps us understand how quickly products form and the factors affecting reaction speed.

Study the speed at which reactants turn into products, measured as the change in concentration over time.

Factors Affecting Reaction Rates

Various factors impact the speed of chemical reactions. Learning about these factors helps control reaction rates in practical applications.

Concentration of Reactants

Higher concentrations of reactants usually increase reaction rates due to more frequent collisions.

Catalysts

Catalysts accelerate reactions without being consumed by providing an alternative pathway with lower activation energy.

Surface Area

Increasing the surface area of solid reactants boosts reaction rate by allowing more contact between reactants.

Pressure

In gases, increasing pressure raises the reaction rate by forcing gas molecules closer together, resulting in more frequent collisions.
