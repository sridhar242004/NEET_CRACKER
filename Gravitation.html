Gravitation

Explore the fundamental force of gravity and its role in the universe, essential for NEET preparation.

Understanding Gravitation

Gravitation is a natural phenomenon by which objects with mass are attracted to each other. This force governs the motion of planets, stars, and galaxies in the universe.

Newton's Law of Gravitation

The universal law that states that every mass attracts every other mass with a force that is proportional to their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

Gravitational Force

Understanding the force of attraction between two masses and how it influences celestial bodies.

Gravitational Potential Energy

Energy possessed by an object due to its position in a gravitational field, crucial in analyzing motion in space.

Acceleration Due to Gravity

The constant acceleration experienced by an object in free fall near the Earth's surface, approximately 9.8 m/s².

Orbital Mechanics

Gravitation plays a crucial role in keeping celestial bodies in orbit. Explore the principles governing orbits and motion in space.

Kepler's Laws of Planetary Motion

Three fundamental laws that describe the motion of planets around the sun, detailing their orbits and relative speeds.

Escape Velocity

The minimum speed needed for an object to escape the gravitational pull of a celestial body.

Satellites and Orbits

Understanding the orbits of artificial satellites and the forces that keep them in motion around planets.

Weightlessness and Free Fall

Exploring the phenomenon of weightlessness in space and the effects of free-fall on objects and astronauts.
