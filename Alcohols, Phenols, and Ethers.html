Introduction to Aldehydes and Ketones

Aldehydes and ketones are organic compounds containing the carbonyl group. They differ in structure but share similarities in reactivity and properties.

Structure of Aldehydes

Contains a carbonyl group (C=O) attached to a hydrogen atom and an alkyl or aryl group. Common in natural flavors and fragrances.

Structure of Ketones

Contains a carbonyl group bonded to two alkyl or aryl groups. Used widely in solvents and pharmaceuticals.

Physical Properties

Explore boiling points, solubility, and other physical properties influenced by the carbonyl group.

Chemical Reactions

Study key reactions like nucleophilic addition, oxidation, and reduction.

Carboxylic Acids

Carboxylic acids contain a carboxyl group (–COOH) and are known for their acidic properties. They play essential roles in biochemistry and industrial applications.

Structure and Nomenclature

Understand the structure of carboxylic acids and learn naming conventions for simple and complex acids.

Acidity and Properties

Explore factors affecting acidity, such as inductive and resonance effects, and the physical properties of carboxylic acids.

Preparation of Carboxylic Acids

Learn about various methods for synthesizing carboxylic acids, including oxidation of aldehydes and alkyl benzenes.

Chemical Reactions

Study important reactions, including esterification, reduction, and substitution at the α-carbon.
